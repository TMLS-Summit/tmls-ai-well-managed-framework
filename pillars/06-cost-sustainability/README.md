# Pillar 6: Cost & Sustainability

> **Focus**: Transparency, lock-in risk, long-term viability

## Overview

True cost extends beyond initial implementation. This pillar evaluates **cost transparency, vendor lock-in risk, and long-term sustainability** to ensure systems remain viable and economically sound throughout their lifecycle.

## Core Question

**Do we understand the total cost of ownership, can we change vendors if needed, and is this system sustainable long-term?**

## Key Principles

### 1. Cost Transparency
- All costs visible and attributable (infrastructure, licenses, operations, support)
- Cost allocation by business unit, project, or service
- Forecasting and budgeting based on usage patterns

### 2. Vendor Independence
- Avoid architectural decisions that create irreversible dependencies
- Use open standards and portable formats
- Maintain exit strategies for critical vendor relationships

### 3. Right-Sizing
- Match resources to actual needs, not theoretical maximums
- Regular optimization reviews
- Auto-scaling and cost-aware architecture

### 4. Technical Debt Management
- Track and prioritize technical debt
- Balance feature velocity with system maintainability
- Allocate capacity for refactoring and modernization

### 5. Environmental Sustainability
- Energy-efficient architecture choices
- Carbon footprint awareness and reduction
- Sustainable scaling practices

## Evaluation Criteria

**Executive Checklist**:
- [ ] Is total cost of ownership documented and tracked?
- [ ] Can costs be attributed to business units or projects?
- [ ] Are usage trends monitored for forecasting?
- [ ] Can the system be migrated to another vendor if needed?
- [ ] Are open standards used where possible?
- [ ] Is technical debt quantified and managed?
- [ ] Are resources right-sized for actual usage?
- [ ] Is there a cost optimization review process?
- [ ] Is environmental impact considered in architecture decisions?
- [ ] Are licensing terms sustainable long-term?

## Common Failure Patterns

❌ **Invisible Costs**: Hidden operational or licensing costs discovered too late  
❌ **Vendor Lock-In**: Architecture decisions that make switching impossible  
❌ **Over-Provisioning**: Resources allocated for peak that rarely occurs  
❌ **Technical Debt Spiral**: Accumulating debt makes change increasingly expensive  
❌ **Sticker Price Fallacy**: Comparing only purchase price, not total cost of ownership  
❌ **Zombie Resources**: Unused resources that continue to incur costs  
❌ **Short-Term Optimization**: Decisions that save money today but increase long-term cost

## Cross-Pillar Dependencies

- **Business Alignment**: ROI requires cost transparency
- **Operational Excellence**: Efficient operations reduce costs
- **Reliability & Resilience**: Over-engineering increases cost without proportional value
- **Compliance & Governance**: Compliance costs must be factored into TCO

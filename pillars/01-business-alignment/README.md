# Pillar 1: Business Alignment

> **Focus**: Linkage to outcomes, ownership, and ROI

## Overview

Every system must demonstrate clear linkage to business outcomes. This pillar evaluates whether ownership is defined, ROI is measurable, and strategic fit is documented. **Systems without business justification represent unmanaged risk.**

## Core Question

**Does this system have a clear business owner, measurable outcomes, and documented strategic value?**

## Key Principles

### 1. Clear Ownership
- Every system must have a designated business owner
- Ownership accountability extends beyond IT to business units
- Decision rights and escalation paths must be documented

### 2. Measurable ROI
- Value must be quantifiable (revenue, cost reduction, risk mitigation)
- Success metrics should be defined before implementation
- Regular value realization reviews should occur

### 3. Strategic Alignment
- Systems should map to strategic business objectives
- Organizational changes required for value capture must be identified
- Investment prioritization should reflect strategic importance

### 4. Lifecycle Management
- Total cost of ownership includes maintenance, evolution, and decommission
- Sunsetting criteria should be defined at inception
- Systems should not outlive their business justification

## Evaluation Criteria

**Executive Checklist**:
- [ ] Is there a named business owner with authority and accountability?
- [ ] Can the system's value be expressed in business metrics?
- [ ] Is there a documented business case with ROI projections?
- [ ] Are success metrics defined and actively tracked?
- [ ] Does the system align with strategic objectives?
- [ ] Are organizational change requirements identified?
- [ ] Is there a plan for value realization and adoption?
- [ ] Are decommission criteria defined?

## Common Failure Patterns

❌ **Technology-First Thinking**: Building systems because technology is available, not because business needs exist  
❌ **Orphaned Systems**: No clear owner after initial deployment  
❌ **Vanity Metrics**: Measuring activity rather than business outcomes  
❌ **Scope Creep**: Initial business case no longer reflects current reality  
❌ **Zombie Systems**: Systems that continue to consume resources without delivering value

## Cross-Pillar Dependencies

- **Compliance & Governance**: Business ownership enables policy enforcement
- **Cost & Sustainability**: ROI tracking requires cost transparency
- **Operational Excellence**: Business metrics inform operational priorities

# TMLS Well-Architected Framework

> A vendor-neutral standard for evaluating modern digital and AI systems

## Overview

The **TMLS Well-Architected Framework** provides a clear, defensible reference point for organizations deploying AI, cloud, and digital systems. It empowers boards and executives to evaluate whether systems are safe, compliant, resilient, and worth scaling.

### The Core Question

> **Is this system safe, sustainable, compliant, and worth scaling?**

This framework answers this critical question through comprehensive evaluation across seven essential pillars, providing clarity without oversimplification.

## The Seven Pillars

The framework evaluates systems across seven critical dimensions:

| Pillar | Focus Area |
|--------|-----------|
| **[Business Alignment](./pillars/01-business-alignment/)** | Linkage to outcomes, ownership, and ROI |
| **[Security & Trust](./pillars/02-security-trust/)** | Data protection, misuse prevention, accountability |
| **[Reliability & Resilience](./pillars/03-reliability-resilience/)** | Failure handling and recovery readiness |
| **[Compliance & Governance](./pillars/04-compliance-governance/)** | Auditability, policy enforcement, legal defensibility |
| **[Operational Excellence](./pillars/05-operational-excellence/)** | Observability, response, human oversight |
| **[Cost & Sustainability](./pillars/06-cost-sustainability/)** | Transparency, lock-in risk, long-term viability |
| **[AI-Specific Responsibility](./pillars/07-ai-responsibility/)** | Model governance, data lineage, human control |

Each pillar includes principles, executive checklists, and common failure patterns to ensure evaluation is comprehensive, repeatable, and aligned with board-level risk oversight.

## Why This Framework?

### Vendor-Neutral by Design
- **Neutral Evaluation Criteria**: Principles applicable across all platforms and architectures
- **Cross-Industry Applicability**: Relevant for finance, healthcare, manufacturing, and government
- **Focus on Judgment**: Emphasizes trade-offs and outcomes rather than specific implementations

### Proven Model
Drawing inspiration from the success of the AWS Well-Architected Framework, TMLS applies the same proven model to AI, security, and modern digital systems—without vendor dependence.

### Key Benefits
✅ **Common Language**: Establishes shared vocabulary between technical teams and leadership  
✅ **Influence Without Mandate**: Guides architecture decisions without dictating specific tools  
✅ **Ecosystem Growth**: Enables education, certification, and broader industry participation

## The Governance Gap

Organizations are deploying AI and digital systems faster than they can govern them. The challenge is no longer access to technology or expertise, but the **absence of a shared, executive-level standard** for evaluating whether systems are safe, compliant, resilient, and worth scaling.

This framework provides that standard.

## Mission

To establish a vendor-neutral, expert-curated standard that empowers boards and executives to ask the right questions, assess risk consistently, and demonstrate due diligence in an era of accelerating technological change.
